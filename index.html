About HumaniTill

A symbol of hope and humanity

What is HumaniTill?

HumaniTill is an innovative CSR initiative by Maliban Biscuit Manufactories as part of the Jeewithaloka program. This life-sized, human-shaped transparent donation box serves as a powerful symbol of missing limbs and the hope for restoration.

Why it Matters

In Sri Lanka, thousands of individuals live without prosthetic limbs due to financial constraints. HumaniTill bridges this gap by collecting donations from the public and channeling them directly to those in need through our partnership with the Meththa Foundation.

Who Benefits

Our initiative primarily supports individuals who have lost limbs due to accidents, illness, or conflict, providing them with high-quality prosthetic limbs free of charge to restore their mobility and independence.

Project Highlights

How HumaniTill makes a difference

1

Conception & Design

The transparent human-shaped till was designed to symbolize missing limbs and create visual impact, encouraging donations from pilgrims and visitors.

2

Partnership with Meththa Foundation

Strategic collaboration established to ensure donations reach beneficiaries and high-quality prosthetic limbs are provided to those in need.

3

Poson Poya Launch - June 2025

Official launch on June 10-11, 2025 during the sacred Poson Poya pilgrimage at Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi and Ruwanwelisaya in Anuradhapura, reaching thousands of devotees.

4

Nationwide Expansion

HumaniTill installations expanded to multiple locations across Sri Lanka, including temples, shopping centers, and community events.
